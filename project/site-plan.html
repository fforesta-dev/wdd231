The Plan of Salvation: Our Eternal Journey – Site Plan

WDD 231 – Individual Project

1. Site Name

Site Name: The Plan of Salvation: Our Eternal Journey

Reason for this name: This name is both spiritual and doctrinal, clearly identifying the website’s focus on the Plan of Salvation while adding an uplifting, engaging tone with the phrase “Our Eternal Journey.” It reflects the idea that each person is progressing along a divine path planned by a loving Heavenly Father, making the site inviting for members, new converts, youth, and anyone learning about the restored gospel.

2. Site Purpose

“The Plan of Salvation: Our Eternal Journey” is an interactive and educational website designed to help visitors understand God’s eternal plan in a clear and uplifting way. The site presents step-by-step explanations, visual diagrams, scriptures, and short reflections that guide learners through each part of the Plan of Salvation. The goal is to provide a spiritually meaningful resource that teaches doctrine while inviting personal reflection on our eternal journey back to God.

This three-page website will present an interactive and educational overview of the Plan of Salvation. It will include clear explanations, visual diagrams, and user-friendly sections that help visitors understand the doctrine step-by-step.

The site will:

  • Explain each part of the Plan of Salvation in simple, accessible language.
  • Provide a visual diagram of the plan to support different learning styles.
  • Include scripture references and quotations from modern prophets.
  • Offer short interactive elements such as a modal dialog with extra insights.
  • Use JSON data to load and display key principles (for example, stages of the plan with titles, short descriptions, and scriptures).
  • Invite visitors to reflect on what the Plan of Salvation means personally through a small form or prompt.

The goal is to provide an engaging learning resource that is accessible on all devices, visually uplifting, and spiritually focused.

3. Project Reason and Personal Motivation

I chose this subject because I want to create something meaningful that aligns with my faith, my mission service, and my desire to help others understand gospel principles in a simple way. This topic motivates me personally, and I already have ideas, scriptures, and content that will allow me to build a complete and authentic website without placeholders.

Creating this site will also strengthen my frontend development skills by combining design, accessibility, and dynamic functionality with something spiritually valuable. It lets me practice planning, responsive layout, modals, JSON data, and forms while building a website that I would actually want to share in a church or study context.

4. Scenarios (User Questions)

These scenarios represent questions that the target audience (youth, new members, investigators, and gospel learners) might ask when visiting the site:

  • Scenario 1: “I am new to the Church. What is the Plan of Salvation, and how do all the parts (premortal life, earth life, spirit world, resurrection, and kingdoms of glory) fit together?”
  • Scenario 2: “Where can I find simple explanations and scriptures that I can use to teach a friend or family member about the Plan of Salvation?”
  • Scenario 3: “How does the Plan of Salvation apply to my everyday choices and my hope in Jesus Christ?”

5. Color Scheme

The colors are chosen to feel calm, spiritual, and uplifting, with good contrast for readability.

Primary Color

Hex: #1A365D

Use: Site header background, navigation bar, main headings, buttons.

Accent Color

Hex: #F6AD55

Use: Call-to-action buttons, highlights, borders, important icons.

Background Color

Hex: #F7FAFC

Use: Page background to keep the site light, soft, and readable.

Text Color

Hex: #1A202C

Use: Body text and most paragraphs for strong contrast on the light background.

This site plan document already uses these colors for the header, headings, accents, and background to match the planned website.

6. Typography

The typography is chosen to feel readable, friendly, and appropriate for gospel content.

  • Headings: Merriweather, serif Used for the site title, section headings <h1>, <h2>, and any scripture/quote headings.
  • Body Text: Nunito, sans-serif Used for paragraphs, lists, captions, and general content to keep reading comfortable.

These fonts are loaded from Google Fonts and are already applied in this site plan document to demonstrate how they will look on the final website.

7. Home Page Wireframe

The home page will be responsive, with a layout that adapts to both mobile and larger viewports. The diagrams below are simple wireframes created with CSS boxes to represent the planned structure.

Mobile Wireframe (Small Viewport)

Header / Logo / Navigation
Hero Section – Title + short intro + CTA button
Section 1 – Overview of the Plan of Salvation
Section 2 – Timeline / Diagram (scrollable)
Section 3 – Scripture Insights (modal trigger)
Section 4 – Reflection Form / “What does this mean for me?”
Footer – Links, small quote, contact/info

Desktop / Tablet Wireframe (Larger Viewport)

Header with Logo, Site Title, Navigation
Hero – Title, short intro, main image/graphic, CTA
Section 1 – Overview text
Section 2 – Plan of Salvation diagram
Section 3 – Scripture insights (modal link)
Section 4 – Reflection form / prompts
Footer – Scripture quote, small links, copyright

Actual images, icons, and final copy will be added during the build phase in Week 6, but the structure of the home page will follow this basic layout.

8. Validation and Testing Plan

Before submitting the final version of this site plan and later the full project, I will:

  • Run the HTML through the W3C Markup Validator.
  • Check accessibility with the WAVE Evaluation Tool.
  • Use Chrome DevTools Lighthouse or PageSpeed Insights to review performance, SEO, and best practices.
  • Verify color contrast with the WebAIM Contrast Checker.
